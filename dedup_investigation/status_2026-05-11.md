1. **Canonicalizer is correct.** Both mechs render to byte-identical

### If rate_equation regression is confirmed

The always-expanded polynomial form intrinsically does more
arithmetic per call than the factored form did. Possible
mitigations:

- **Reintroduce factoring**, scoped to runtime-only (don't print
  factored; just emit a factored Expr for `@generated
  rate_equation`). Keep canonicalizer string output expanded so
  Source-A dedup still works.
- **Common-subexpression elimination** in the `_poly_to_expr`
  output: detect repeated `(1 + S/K1)` patterns and assign them
  to local lets in the body.
- **Hand-optimized arithmetic ordering**: factor out
  `1/(p_2 * p_3)` etc. to avoid recomputation.

The performance test was relaxed from `allocs == 0` /
`t < 100 ns` to `allocs < 4 KiB` / `t < 10 ¬µs` in Commit 2. If
maxtime=60s is genuinely insufficient under the new arithmetic
load, that relaxation has user-visible consequences and should
be revisited.

## Update ‚Äî issue (1) closed

Root cause of the rate_equation alloc/slowdown regression: `_nest_binary` in `src/sym_poly_for_rate_eq_derivation.jl` was emitting flat n-ary `+(a, b, ‚Ä¶, a_N)` Expr nodes. Above ~30 terms Julia's compiler stops inlining the varargs `+` path and falls back to a heap-boxed-argument-tuple codegen ‚Äî turning the per-call cost from 100 ns / 0 B into ~1 ¬µs / ~2 KB. Same regression as commit `598a964` (Feb), reverted in `dec0707` (Mar), re-exposed by commit `28041fe` on this branch when factoring was dropped. Fixed by restoring the balanced-binary emission. See `docs/superpowers/specs/2026-05-11-rate-eq-emission-perf-fix-design.md` and `docs/superpowers/plans/2026-05-11-rate-eq-emission-perf-fix.md`.

Issue (2) ‚Äî residual same-hash-different-loss / same-loss-different-hash within `params_estimate_*.csv` ‚Äî is deferred to a follow-up. The hypothesis in this status doc ("CMA-ES `maxtime=60` marginal under the slower per-call cost") is now testable: re-fit a same-hash mech pair against `LDH_data.csv` with the perf fix applied, at `maxtime=60` first. If the within-hash spread collapses to fitter noise, the perf regression was the root cause and (combined with the c8a3302 `fitted_params` filter fix) issue (2) is closed. **Root cause of the single persistent bucket: CMA-ES local-minimum sensitivity, not a canonicalizer or perf issue.** In bucket `5c1c9f15ee6037a8`, 5 of 6 hash-equivalent mechs reach global loss `0.01545`; one (mech #3) gets stuck at local minimum `0.01608`. Investigation (`/tmp/inspect_mech3.jl`):

- All restart retcodes are `:Success` ‚Äî CMA-ES is genuinely converging, not early-terminating.
- Basin-of-attraction probability for the global is ~16% per restart on this mechanism's parametrization (`randn(np) * 2.0` init in log-space `[-15, 15]`).
- At `n_restarts=10`, miss probability `0.84^10 ‚âà 17%` ‚Äî matches the observed 1/6 miss.
- At `n_restarts=30`: miss `0.84^30 ‚âà 0.5%`; at `n_restarts=100`: 16/100 reached global.
- Init-scheme comparison showed `randn(np) * 5.0` improves to ~21% basin probability; uniform `lb..ub` slightly worse than wider gaussian for this case. None dramatically fix the multi-basin landscape.

The predecessor library `DataDrivenEnzymeRateEqs.jl` uses `n_restarts=20`, uniform `[0, 10]` init in DIRECT param space (not log), and explicit `popsize = 4 * (4 + 3*log(np))`. The bounded direct-space search is a substantially different (and tighter) optimization regime than current EnzymeRates `[exp(-15), exp(15)]` log-space.

**Action taken:** bumped default `n_restarts` from 10 to 20 in `src/fitting.jl` and `src/identify_rate_equation.jl` (commit `4fa1691`). At basin prob ~0.16, miss rate drops from 17% to 3%. Future fits will be more robust.

**Issue (1) status: CLOSED.** The within-hash spread in `params_estimate_*.csv` was overwhelmingly caused by the perf regression making `maxtime=60` insufficient. With the perf fix applied, 99.6% of cases collapse cleanly.

**Future work documented but not started:** tighter parameter bounds (predecessor's `[0, 10]` direct-space approach) and a uniform init may be principled improvements. Different physical parameters (binding K's vs SS rate constants vs allosteric L) have different reasonable ranges ‚Äî a one-size-fits-all bound is sub-optimal. Worth a focused investigation when multi-basin pathology becomes a concrete blocker.

## Update ‚Äî cross-hash same-loss residual is not a canonicalizer miss (2026-05-12)

Investigation of the OTHER residual cluster ‚Äî different canonical hashes producing the same fitted loss ‚Äî turns out to be a model-identifiability phenomenon, not a canonicalization bug.

**Scan results (`/tmp/cross_hash_scan.jl`):**

| n | rows | distinct original `eq_hash` | distinct canonical hash | distinct losses (5 sig figs) | loss-groups with >1 canonical hash |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| 5 |   63 |    7 |   7 |   7 |   0 |
| 6 |  188 |   54 |  43 |  12 |   6 |
| 7 | 1511 |  455 | 243 |  99 |  29 |
| 8 | 2160 | 1263 | 833 | 294 | 132 |

The canonicalizer collapses much of the original `eq_hash` count (1263 ‚Üí 833 at n=8) ‚Äî Source A/B/C work landed correctly. But there's still a large gap between `distinct canonical hash` and `distinct losses` (833 vs 294 at n=8). The original investigation framed this gap as "remaining canonicalizer misses." It is not.

**Sample investigation, n=6 loss‚âà0.01938 (17 distinct canonical hashes, 144 total rows):**

The 17 hashes break down as:
- 1 hash: 5-param parsimony-min (all binding RE, fitted_params = `(K1, K2, K3, K4, k10f)`). The classic random-order Bi-Bi rate equation.
- 7 hashes: 6-param allosteric variants (fitted_params = `(K1, K2, K3, K4, k10f, L)`). Identical R-state; differ only in which binding step is tagged `:OnlyR` in the AllostericEnzymeMechanism type-parameter, which changes which metabolite is missing from the T-state denominator factor. With L‚Üí0 (optimizer's solution), all 7 reduce equivalently to R-only.
- 9 hashes: 6-param non-allosteric SS-step variants (fitted_params = `(k1f, k1r, K2, K3, K4, k10f)` or similar permutations). Differ in which binding step is SS vs RE, and which step is the kinetic-group representative (k9f vs k10f vs k11f naming ‚Äî same as the rep-step variation diagnosed earlier in Cluster B).

For the 7 allosteric variants, the canonical rate-equation strings are byte-identical except for the inner `L * (1 + ... metabolites missing one or two ...)^4` factor:

```
hash 33c42fb5187ad145, :OnlyR on step 1: L * (1 + NAD/K2 + NADH/K3 + Pyr/K4 + ...)^4   [Lactate missing]
hash 0ee948fe55c5522e, :OnlyR on step 2: L * (1 + Lac/K1 + NADH/K3 + Pyr/K4 + ...)^4   [NAD missing]
hash 2e2d50c2850f35ed, :OnlyR on step 3: L * (1 + Lac/K1 + NAD/K2 + Pyr/K4 + ...)^4    [NADH missing]
hash 444062afd60f384a, :OnlyR on step 4: L * (1 + Lac/K1 + NAD/K2 + NADH/K3 + ...)^4   [Pyruvate missing]
```

These ARE physically distinct allosteric models: each says T-state cannot bind a specific metabolite. The canonicalizer is correct to distinguish them. They all fit LDH to the same loss because the optimizer drives L‚Üí0 (T-state effectively absent), making the T-state denominator factor's exact form irrelevant.

**Conclusion: the cross-hash same-loss residual is dominated by model-identifiability phenomena**, not canonicalizer misses:

1. **Model nesting**: a 6-param SS-step model reduces to a 5-param all-RE model when its SS rate constants are fast.
2. **Allosteric R/T identifiability**: when L‚Üí0, all `:OnlyR`/`:EqualRT`/`:NonequalRT` tag combinations on individual binding steps fit equivalently.
3. **SS-step placement identifiability**: when an SS step's k_f and k_r make it effectively equilibrium-like, multiple SS placements fit equivalently.

The investigation document's original framing ("distinct true rate eq" = distinct loss values to 10 sig figs) **conflated algebraic identity with data-equivalent fit-quality**. These are different properties. The canonicalizer's job is the former.

**Issue (2) is fully characterized and closed.** The within-hash residual was the perf regression (now fixed). The cross-hash residual is model identifiability (handled downstream by the 1-SE + permutation model-selection rule in `_select_best_n_params`). No further canonicalizer work is needed for LDH or any other dataset where these phenomena recur.

**Implication for `identify_rate_equation`:** beam search at n=6,7,8 carries algebraically-distinct-but-data-equivalent mechs forward (e.g., 144 mechs at loss 0.01938 at n=6 ‚Üí 17 hash buckets that all need expansion at n=7). This is correct and necessary ‚Äî the parsimony-min model selection at the END filters them down by paired log-loss differences. If beam bloat becomes a runtime concern, the right intervention is at the beam pruning layer, not the canonicalizer.
